Description

The workshop is structured around four core themes of the new Proto-Coptic model and features contributions from invited specialists:

  • First, the phonological proposal argues for a two-vowel system (*a, *E), with Dr. Lameen Souag providing comparative Afro-Asiatic perspectives (Berber/Chadic).
  • Second, a revised account of deverbal nouns replaces traditional categories with five morphosemantic classes; this will be discussed alongside (West-)Semitic parallels by Prof. Ahmad Al-Jallad and in relation to Egyptian root nouns by Dr. Roman Gundacker.
  • Third, the syntactic analysis reinterprets so-called verbal clauses as fundamentally nominal or cleft-like constructions, a view explored through Cushitic evidence by Prof. Maarten Mous and methodological considerations by Prof. Jean Winand.
  • Finally, the workshop addresses the emergence of Egyptian as a contact phenomenon, with contributions from Dr. Roger Blench on Afro-Asiatic etymologies, Prof. Gaëlle Chantrain on Egyptian–Bedja semantic parallels, and Prof. Victoriy Almansa-Villatoro on multilingualism in the Old Kingdom.
