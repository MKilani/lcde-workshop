Description

This workshop presents the results of a four-year research project on Egyptian-Coptic linguistics. Initially focused on dialectal variation in Late Egyptian, the project developed into a broader reassessment of linguistic reconstruction, leading to a new model of Proto-Coptic.

The proposed model introduces significant innovations, offering improved internal coherence and new insights into the historical development of Egyptian and its Afro-Asiatic context.

Themes & Contributions

  • Phonology: Two-vowel system (*a, *E) — Dr. Lameen Souag
  • Deverbal Nouns: Five morphosemantic classes — Prof. Ahmad Al-Jallad, Dr. Roman Gundacker
  • Syntax: Nominal / cleft-like clause structures — Prof. Maarten Mous, Prof. Jean Winand
  • Language Contact: Afro-Asiatic and Egyptian interactions — Dr. Roger Blench, Prof. Gaëlle Chantrain, Prof. Victoriy Almansa-Villatoro
